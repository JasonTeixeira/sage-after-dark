## A heading is a promise

The H2 reframes the section. Each section runs 200–500 words. Don't go longer without a payoff.

Make the argument with scenes and specifics, not abstractions. Show the receipt. End the section with a sentence that earns the next H2.

## The deadpan list

A signature move: the deadpan enumerated list with a thesis baked into the items.

**The thing in category one.** A sentence about why it looks like the answer and isn't.

**The thing in category two.** A sentence about what it actually is.

**The thing in category three.** A sentence about what most people miss about it. This is where the essay's core insight lands.<Sidenote n="2">If you can't write a deadpan list for your topic, you may not have an essay yet — you may have an opinion. They're different.</Sidenote>

## Receipts, not vibes

Use real numbers, real screenshots, real quotes. Inline `code` for terms of art, fenced blocks for actual code.

```ts
// Show the smallest version that proves the point.
function halfLife<T>(items: T[]): T[] {
  return items.filter((it) => stillWorks(it, FIVE_YEARS));
}
```

<FieldCard kind="tool" title="THE FIELD CARD">

A field card breaks the page rhythm. Use it for a tool, a counter-example, a warning, or a principle that deserves a frame. `kind` accepts `tool`, `counter`, `warning`, or `principle`. Keep the body under ~120 words.

</FieldCard>

The paragraph after a field card shouldn't apologize for the field card. Pick up the argument and keep going.

## The pull-quote, used sparingly

Once per post, max. Reserve it for the sentence you'd put on a t-shirt.

<PullQuote attribution="— the working title of this essay">
The one sentence that names the thing.
</PullQuote>

Keep moving. Don't admire your own pull quote.

## What this means tomorrow

Before the diagnostic, give the reader a concrete claim about how the world looks now that they've read this. One paragraph. No hedging.

<Diagnostic

    "The second thing — slightly harder, slightly more specific.",
    "The third thing — the one that requires the change in framing the essay just argued for.",
    "The fourth, optional. Cut to three if four is filler."
  ]}
/>

The signoff is one line. Quiet. It belongs to the essay, not to the next one.
