## The shape of the system

Before we get into specifics, the meta-shape: I run two parallel tracks every day. Track one is the *ship* track — the work that has a deliverable, a deadline, and a customer waiting. Track two is the *write* track — the writing, the dispatches, the long-form work that compounds over years instead of weeks. Both tracks have their own tools, their own rituals, and their own definition of "done."

The mistake I see most people make is trying to run one system for both tracks. Ship work is bursty, deadline-driven, and tolerant of mess. Write work is metronomic, internal, and intolerant of interruption. The same calendar block, the same task list, the same focus method does not serve both. I learned this the hard way over five years of "productivity systems" that worked for half of my life and broke the other half.
