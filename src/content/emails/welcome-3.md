Quick one.

Three days in, and I've been thinking about which of the four anchor essays to actually nudge you toward — and the honest answer is **[Taste as a Deploy Gate](https://www.sageafterdark.com/taste/taste-as-a-deploy-gate)**.

Here's why.

Most of what gets called "engineering judgment" is just taste — the unconscious filter that catches the thing that *passes review but shouldn't ship*. The interface that's technically correct but feels off. The error message that's accurate but condescending. The default that's defensible but lazy.

Taste is the deploy gate that runs after lint, type, and test. It either fires or it doesn't. And when it fires, the cost of overriding it is almost always higher than the cost of fixing it.

The essay is about how to keep that filter sharp when you're shipping fast — and what to do when you don't trust your own taste yet.

It's a 12-minute read. There's a checklist at the end you can actually steal.
