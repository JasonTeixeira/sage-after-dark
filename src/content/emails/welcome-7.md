A week in. Thank you for sticking around — it's the small list, and I notice every name on it.

Three things you might not know yet:

**1. There are story arcs.**
Long-form work doesn't fit in a single essay, so I publish in **arcs** — multi-part series that build on each other. Three are active right now:

- **Trayd in Public** — building a construction-payments product as a one-person studio, with the numbers.
- **The Slow Internet** — what we're losing as the algorithmic feed eats the open web, and what to build instead.
- **Operator's Field Manual** — the unglamorous craft notes from shipping software solo.

[See all arcs →](https://www.sageafterdark.com/#arcs)

**2. You can subscribe to just one pillar.**
Don't want every essay? Each pillar has its own RSS feed — `/build/feed.xml`, `/signal/feed.xml`, `/world/feed.xml`, etc. Plug it into Reader, Feedly, NetNewsWire — whatever you use. The Sunday letter still ties everything together.

**3. The members tier is opening soon.**
Founding subscribers (you) will keep everything free, get a permanent founding-member badge, and a one-time invitation when the paid tier launches. There's no upsell pressure — it just exists for readers who want the long versions of dispatches and the members-only tutorials. More on that in a future letter.

That's it for the welcome series. From here on it's just the Sunday transmission, and the occasional Wednesday field note when the week produces something worth saying out loud.

See you Sunday.
